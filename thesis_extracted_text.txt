Background of the Study 
Barangay Health Centers in the Philippines still rely heavily on handwritten 
records for immunization and patient information. While these documents remain 
the official source of health data, they are increasingly inefficient and prone to 
errors. Handwritten records can be difficult to read, misplaced, or damaged over 
time, while manual searching consumes valuable time that health workers could 
otherwise spend on patient care (Katurura & Cilliers, 2018). In barangay health 
centers, some forms and tables are  printed. But most patient records and 
vaccination logs are written by hand. This mixture makes traditional record-keeping 
more prone to inconsistencies and harder to manage. To address these 
challenges, this study proposes the development of a digital hea lth record 
management system that integrates Optical Character Recognition (OCR), Natural 
Language Processing (NLP), Image Processing, and Cloud Integration. OCR will 
be used to recognize both handwritten entries and printed elements, while NLP will 
enable intelligent classification and retrieval. Image processing will enhance the 
clarity of scanned documents, and cloud integ ration will ensure secure and 
accessible storage.  
Studies show that paper files are vulnerable to misplacement, deterioration, 
and environmental damage, making long -term preservation difficult. In addition, 
handwritten records have been associated with issues such as unclear writing, 
incompleteness, inconsistent terminology, and difficulty in retrieval (Akhu -Zaheya

record management system for barangay health centers . The study specifically 
aims to address the following objectives:

extract and digitize information from manually written vaccination and 
medical entries. 
2. To process OCR-generated vaccination record data using TF-IDF and SVM 
for automated component classification and to apply BioBERT embeddings 
for semantic retrieval of vaccination information.

Scope and Limitations of the Project  
The scope of this study centers on the design, development, and 
implementation of a digital health record management system intended for 
barangay health centers, with a primary focus on automating the digitization and 
retrieval of child vaccination records . The system integrates Optical Character 
Recognition (OCR) and Natural Language Processing (NLP) techniques to extract 
handwritten vaccination entries, classify relevant information, and support efficient 
digital access to immunization data. Its overarchi ng purpose is to replace manual 
record handling with a more organized and intelligent digital platform that supports 
community-level vaccination monitoring. 
A major component of the study’s scope is the collection and preparation of 
240 handwritten vaccination records, which serve as the primary data source for 
OCR processing. The study will gather actual child immunization records from 
selected barangay health centers containing handwritten entries such as vaccine

 Natural Language Processing  - a branch of artificial intelligence (AI) and

Optical Character Recognition- Text-image conversion technique that converts

Fine-Tuning - entails techniques to further train a model whose weights have 
already been updated through prior training. Using the base model’s previous 
knowledge as a starting point, fine -tuning tailors the model by training it on a 
smaller, task-specific dataset.

This chapter's goal is to provide a thorough analysis of academic

Digital Transformation in Community Health Records 
Automation of information extraction has been transformed by the 
development of artificial intelligence and machine learning, especially in the 
healthcare industry where accuracy, efficiency, and data accessibility are critical. 
Barangay health centers' tr aditional recordkeeping procedures mostly rely on 
printed forms and handwritten vaccination logs, which are prone to human error, 
loss, and damage. A revolutionary framework for digitizing such handwritten 
medical records is offered by the combination of o ptical character recognition

Development of Optical Character Recognition 
This change has been largely attributed to recent developments in OCR 
algorithms. The character-matching heuristics and rule -based templates used by 
early OCR systems did not perform well on handwritten or damaged documents. 
To automatically learn textual and spatial patterns from raw pixel data, modern 
OCR frameworks instead use deep learning models, such as transformer -based 
architectures, recurrent neural networks (RNNs), and convolutional neural 
networks (CNNs) (Parikshit Sharma, 2023; Parth Hasmukh Jai n et al, 2023). 
Because of its Long Short -Term Memory (LSTM) engine, which captures 
sequential dependencies and increases recognition accuracy, Tesseract OCR is 
still a popular open -source system for printed text (Sporici, Cușnir, & Boiangiu, 
2020). According to comparative studies, PaddleOCR, a CNN-Transformer hybrid 
architecture, performs better on both cursive and free -form handwriting (Parth 
Hasmukh Jain et al., 2023). Cloud -based engines like Google Cloud Vision and

The importance of image preprocessing as a factor influencing recognition

NLP for Classification, Retrieval, and Interpretation of OCR Output 
Natural Language Processing (NLP) adds a higher level of intelligence 
beyond text extraction by making it possible to classify, retrieve, and interpret the 
digital data. Unstructured OCR outputs frequently contain errors, misreads, or 
unnecessary text. Using models like Term Frequency –Inverse Document 
Frequency (TF-IDF), which find pertinent keywords and prioritize documents, NLP 
techniques turn this unstructured data into information that can be searched (Parth 
Hasmukh Jain, 2023). The retrieval of domain -specific terms is improved by 
advanced transform er-based models like BioBERT, which further capture 
contextual meaning in medical text (Parikshit Sharma, 2023). Studies show that 
combining natural language processing (NLP) with optical character recognition 
(OCR) greatly improves the searchability and u sability of medical records. For 
example, health professionals can use conversational queries like "Find all 
children who missed their second dose of polio vaccine" to retrieve information. 
The literature also emphasizes how OCR accuracy has a direct impac t on NLP 
performance, as low recognition accuracy can skew entity extraction and search 
relevance (Geeta S. Hukkeri et al., 2021). The majority of OCR -NLP frameworks 
use fine -tuning based on localized datasets or post -processing corrections to 
lessen this. In order to better identify Filipino handwriting styles and contextual 
terminology, the current study intends to pre-train and refine the OCR model using 
actual barangay health records.

Cloud-Based Health Data Storage and System Integration 
The next significant integration dimension is the creation of a digital health 
record management system that is cloud-enabled. Cloud platforms have emerged 
as a key component of medical informatics in recent years because of their 
capacity to facilitate re al-time patient information access, enhance collaboration, 
and unify disparate data sources. According to studies on cloud integration 
architecture, multi -cloud environments foster interoperability and resilience by 
enabling systems to function across providers while maintaining data security and 
redundancy (Cristescu-Pompiliu et al., 2021). Cloud integration reduces the need 
for on -site servers, lowers maintenance costs, and shields data from 
environmental factors that could cause physical loss for health centers with limited 
resources (Muntean et al., 2021). To protect sensitive health data and preserve 
integrity, modern systems use encrypted connections and role -based access 
control (RBAC) (View of Which OCR Toolset is Good and Why, 2021). The design 
goals of the suggested health record system, which include automated record 
classification, secure login authentication, and centralized cloud storage for 
scanned and digital files, are directly supported by these principles. In addition to 
modernizing recordkeeping, the system supports the Department of Health's long-
term objective of establishing national digital health information systems by fusing 
OCR, NLP, and cloud technologies.

GAP BRIDGE OF THE STUDY 
Despite the substantial progress in OCR and NLP research, several gaps 
persist that hinder the application of these technologies to community health record 
systems—gaps that this study aims to bridge. 
First, while OCR algorithms have achieved high accuracy on standard 
printed datasets, their performance on handwritten and cursive text remains 
inconsistent, especially when dealing with low-resolution scans and mixed-format

documents. The literature primarily evaluates Western handwriting datasets, which 
differ significantly from the handwriting styles and linguistic structures found in 
Filipino health records. Thus, there is a lack of OCR models fine-tuned for localized 
and bilingual healthcare data. The present study addresses this gap by fine-tuning 
PaddleOCR using actual barangay immunization health records to improve 
recognition accuracy on Filipino handwriting. 
Second, although NLP has proven effective in classifying and retrieving 
textual data, few studies have explored its integration with OCR for healthcare 
record retrieval in local or low-resource environments. Existing NLP models often 
focus on biomedical li terature and English clinical narratives, leaving a gap in 
contextual search systems that support multilingual data and everyday user 
queries. This study bridges that gap by integrating TF-IDF and domain-tuned NLP 
models into the health record management s ystem to support natural language 
search queries, allowing barangay health workers to retrieve information using 
intuitive, conversational phrasing. 
Third, while cloud integration is widely recognized for its advantages in data 
sharing and storage, many existing systems assume consistent high -speed 
connectivity and modern infrastructure. Barangay health centers often experience 
unstable internet access , limiting their ability to rely entirely on cloud -based 
systems. This study introduces a hybrid architecture that allows offline data access 
and synchronization once connectivity is restored, ensuring functionality and 
resilience in rural environments.

therefore, was a mechanis m that could extract text from mixed handwritten and 
printed forms, interpret the extracted information, store it securely, and retrieve it 
efficiently when needed. 
Functional and non -functional requirements were documented during this

allowed the model to learn the visual -textual mappings specific to th e dataset, 
improving character -level accuracy while retaining the robust text -detection 
capabilities of the backbone.

their corresponding ground-truth transcriptions to the model, allowing it to learn the

Phase 6: Model Integration and Deployment 
After completing the training and evaluation of all components through 5 -
fold cross -validation—including the TF -IDF + SVM classification model, the 
PaddleOCR model for OCR processing, and the BioBERT embedding -based 
retrieval module—the final phase centered on integrating these machine learning 
components into a unified cloud-based web application. 
For deployment, the best-performing model configurations identified during 
cross-validation were selected. Rather than deploying models from a single fold, 
the final production models were retrained using the complete 240-record dataset 
to maximize learnin g from all available data, since cross -validation had already 
provided robust performance estimates. This final training incorporated the optimal 
hyperparameters and configurations validated through the cross -validation 
process. 
This integration ensured that each subsystem functions cohesively within a 
single end -to-end digitization and retrieval workflow. All models were deployed 
within the cloud environment to support real -time OCR processing, text 
vectorization, and semantic re trieval. By consolidating the pipeline into the web 
application, the system enables health workers to upload records, access digitized 
data, and retrieve relevant information seamlessly and remotely through an 
accessible browser-based interface.

Hardware Specification 
 The hardware requirements for the Digital Vaccination Records 
Management System are categorized into two primary environments: the 
development machine used during system and model creation, and the production 
server used for deployment and real -world operation. The development machine 
required a moderately high -performance configuration to support system 
development, testing, and the fine -tuning of machine learning components such 
as PaddleOCR. The recommended hardware specification should at least have a 
4-core processor (e.g., AMD Ryzen 7 5700U), 16 GB RAM, and 512 GB SSD to 
support system development and local testing. GPU acceleration for OCR fine -
tuning was recommended through an NVIDIA CUDA -enabled GPU with 6 –8 GB 
VRAM, or alternatively through cloud G PU services such as Google Colab. The 
production server, deployed on Google Cloud Run, required at least 4 vCPUs, 8 –
16 GB RAM, and 50 GB SSD to handle backend services, API processing, and 
model inference. These specifications ensured stable performance fo r OCR 
processing, text classification, and record retrieval during actual system use.
